## Root cause

Cross-module `lru_cache` poisoning between `test_admin_ai.py` and `test_mcp_registry.py` in a full-suite pytest session:

1. pytest alphabetical collection: `test_admin_ai.py` loads first (sets `ENCRYPTION_KEY=KEY_A`), then `test_mcp_registry.py` loads (sets `ENCRYPTION_KEY=KEY_B`).
2. `test_admin_ai.py` autouse teardown (line 335) calls `reset_fernet_cache()` then restores `ENCRYPTION_KEY=KEY_A`. This fires after all admin_ai tests, before mcp_registry tests start.
3. Without the fix, `test_mcp_registry.py::test_T06` ran with `KEY_A` in the env. `_get_fernet()` built `Fernet(KEY_A)` and encrypted the secret; the test expected `Fernet(KEY_B)` for decryption → `InvalidToken`.
4. The fix establishes a two-layer defense (mirrors proven pattern in `test_admin_ai.py:335` and `test_chat_stream.py:124`):
   - Layer 1 (module-level): prevents collection-time cache poisoning.
   - Layer 2 (autouse per-test): prevents execution-time poisoning regardless of what other modules do in teardown.
