### Findings

**F1 — Doc-note `P01-S03-T002-cors-2026-05-13.md` is UNRESOLVED and §Contexto of ADR-002 carries the same SameSite imprecision flagged by researcher D1.**

- Location: `orchestrator-state/memory/official-doc-notes/P01-S03-T002-cors-2026-05-13.md` (no `RESOLVED:` line) AND `docs/source-of-truth/HILO_PEOPLE_TECHNICAL_GUIDE.md` line 615 (ADR-002 Contexto):
  > *"La cookie de refresh (`SameSite=Lax; Path=/api/v1/auth`) sí se envía cross-origin con `credentials:'include'`, pero solo si el preflight pasa."*
- Why this is technically imprecise (per researcher MDN/web.dev citations): `localhost:5173` and `localhost:8000` are **same-site** (port ignored in same-site determination, scheme + eTLD+1 only). `SameSite=Lax` allows the cookie on **same-site XHR/fetch POST** regardless of CORS preflight. The CORS preflight is an **independent** browser mechanism that controls whether JS may issue the request at all; it does not gate cookie inclusion. The phrase "solo si el preflight pasa" conflates the two mechanisms.
- Why it matters: ADR-002 is canonical source-of-truth (§15 append-only ADR log). Future readers (humans + LLM agents reading the pack) will inherit the imprecision and may make wrong reasoning calls about cookie behavior on a future cross-origin slice (e.g. a future mobile/native client decision).
- Severity: low per researcher; non-blocking for the technical conclusion (Strategy A remains correct regardless of how you frame why B was rejected). BUT: the doc-note is procedurally `UNRESOLVED`, which under `.claude/rules/00-source-of-truth.md` and the docs-discrepancy hook contract is a hard blocker for slice close.
- Scope: in-scope (TECHNICAL_GUIDE is in `Write set`; doc-note is the slice's own artifact). NOT an FU.

**Fix — pick ONE of two options (developer's choice):**

- **Option A (preferred, ≤3 lines in ADR-002)** — tighten §Contexto. Replace:
  > *"La cookie de refresh (`SameSite=Lax; Path=/api/v1/auth`) sí se envía cross-origin con `credentials:'include'`, pero solo si el preflight pasa."*
  with:
  > *"`localhost:5173` y `localhost:8000` son same-site (puerto ignorado en la determinación de same-site, solo cuentan scheme + eTLD+1). Por tanto `SameSite=Lax` permite la cookie en XHR same-site entre ellos — el bloqueo real es el preflight CORS (mecanismo independiente de SameSite) que hoy devuelve 405. Strategy A elimina la preflight al colapsar todo a un solo origin."*
  Then add `RESOLVED: ADR-002 §Contexto tightened to use precise same-site language per researcher D1.` to the doc-note.
- **Option B (acceptable, 1 line in the doc-note only)** — add a `RESOLVED:` line explicitly acknowledging the imprecision is preserved with rationale (e.g. *"RESOLVED: ADR-002 conclusion + alternatives + consequences are unaffected by D1; §Contexto preserves the same framing as task-pack §B.3 to keep the historical reasoning chain intact. Future ADR may SUPERSEDE if precision becomes load-bearing."*). This is less ideal because it leaves the imprecision in the canonical doc, but it is permissible under rule 00 (the discrepancy is documented, severity low, decision unaffected).
