- **Before**: `" VALUES (:uid, :eid, :brand, :soc, :ctr, :cntry, :dept, :meta::jsonb)"`

## Rationale

### Why `CAST(:meta AS JSONB)` over `(:meta)::jsonb`
SQLAlchemy's `text()` named-parameter parser uses `:identifier` to locate bind parameters. The expression `:meta::jsonb` (PostgreSQL cast shorthand after a bind param) is ambiguous to the parser in SQLAlchemy 2.x / psycopg3 — the `::` can be misinterpreted as part of the parameter token or as an operator after it, depending on the parser state. The recommended safe forms are `CAST(:meta AS JSONB)` (SQL-standard) or `(:meta)::jsonb` (parenthesized to isolate the bind param). `CAST(:meta AS JSONB)` was chosen for clarity.

### Why `json.dumps()` over `str(...).replace("'", '"')`
The Python `repr` of a dict uses single quotes for strings, `True`/`False` for booleans, and `None` for nulls — all invalid in JSON. `replace("'", '"')` only fixes the string-quote mismatch and silently corrupts booleans and null values. `json.dumps()` is the stdlib serializer for JSON — canonical and correct for all types.
